How to effectively format HTML: The Head

What goes in the Head

There are five elements that should go into your head. 1, the Title, 2, metadata, 3, styles, 4, scripts, and finally 5, links.

Title

The title is the name of your webpage. It's what your page is about, and although it is not visible on your webpage, it is seen by webcrawlers to help categorize the information in the webpage.

Metadata

Similarlly to the title, metadata is also not visible to a viewer on the webpage. However, it still provides extremely important information for searchbots and other web developers. Some of the tags that metadata includes are charset, author, and a brief discription of the webpage.

Styles

Styles are exactly what they sound like. They format webpages and organize the information into a mannar that viewers can easily understand.

Scripts

Scripts such as JavaScript run before your webpage loads. There are other scripts than can be written in at the bottom of your bosy, however any scripts necessary for loading your webpage should be placed in the head.

Links

Links are separate files that are attached/linked to the website. 
  <html lang="en">
    <title>Title of your webpage</title>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width">
    <meta name="author's name" content="short description on the webpage's topic">
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="styles.css">
    <script type="module" src="script.js"></script>
    <style>
    anything to style your webpage for example, centering certain photos
    </style>
  </head>
